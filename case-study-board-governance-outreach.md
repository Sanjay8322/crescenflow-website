## The Problem

Cold LinkedIn outreach is broken. The average response rate for cold LinkedIn DMs is **3–5%**. Most messages are:

- Generic ("I help companies like yours with cybersecurity...")
- Badly timed (no trigger, no urgency)
- Written for a persona, not a person

Board-level executives — Directors, Audit Committee Chairs, CEOs, CFOs — ignore 95%+ of cold outreach because it reads like a pitch, not a conversation between peers.

IpServices needed a way to reach senior board directors at mid-market companies ($50M–$500M revenue) across Healthcare, Financial Services, and Manufacturing — and open conversations about board-level cybersecurity fiduciary exposure — without hiring an SDR team or running expensive paid ads.

---

## The Solution: AI Research-to-Message Pipeline

We built a fully automated outreach pipeline on top of Claude Code that eliminates manual research, writes messages that read like they came from a trusted advisor, and runs QA on every claim before anything gets sent.

### How It Works (4-Step Pipeline)

**Step 1 — Research**  
Each prospect gets a dedicated web research report: current title verification, company recent news, regulatory pressure signals, peer breach events, governance gap indicators, and communication style profile. All sourced and cited.

**Step 2 — Message Generation**  
Based on the research, a personalized LinkedIn DM is written for each prospect. The message:
- Opens with a specific trigger (a regulation they're subject to, a peer breach, a governance gap at their company)
- Never pitches a product — positions as a peer flagging a real exposure
- Ends with a low-friction CTA (assessment link, quick call, or governance snapshot)

**Step 3 — Humanize Check**  
Every message is scanned for AI writing patterns (em-dashes, hedging phrases, corporate jargon, over-structured language) and rewritten in-place where flagged.

**Step 4 — QA Check**  
Every personalization claim is verified: source URL liveness, date freshness (<30 days vs. stale), role still active, claim specific enough to not apply to any other prospect.

The entire pipeline runs from a single command — drop in a CSV of leads, get back ready-to-send HTML messages with copy buttons, char counts, and source links.

## What Made It Work

### 1. Regulatory trigger, not product pitch
Every message opened with a real, sourced regulatory pressure signal — a law the company is subject to, a peer breach in their exact industry, or a governance gap documented by a public regulator. The prospect didn't read a vendor pitch. They read a peer flagging something they're probably not on top of.

### 2. Board-specific framing
The message spoke to fiduciary duty, not technology. Board Directors are lawyers, CFOs, and former CEOs — they think in terms of liability, Caremark doctrine, D&O insurance, and SEC filings. The message met them in that language.

### 3. Zero-friction offer
"Want me to send the free assessment link?" requires no commitment. No "hop on a call," no "schedule a demo." The board.itpi.org assessment is the CTA — self-serve, instant, and genuinely useful on its own.

### 4. Speed at scale without sacrificing quality
23 messages sent on Day 1. Each one backed by 15–20 sources, verified claims, and a communication style profile of the individual. That ratio of depth-to-speed is only possible with the AI research pipeline.

### 5. The sender
All messages went from Scott Aldridge's LinkedIn — CEO of IpServices, President of ITPI. A peer-to-peer message from an executive talking to another executive lands differently than an SDR cold DM.
